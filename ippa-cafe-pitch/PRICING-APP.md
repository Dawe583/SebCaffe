# Ippa app — pricing analysis & what to quote

*Prepared for SiteSpot, 2026-07-18, using the `market-research` skill. All Kč figures use ≈ 23 Kč / USD and are **from-prices** (scope-dependent), not fixed quotes.*

---

## 1. Executive summary

The Ippa app (order-ahead + pickup, loyalty, course & cake booking, gift cards/wallet, push, 4 branches, 3 languages, payments) is a **mid-range, multi-feature** build — above a "basic single-restaurant app," below an enterprise/delivery-logistics platform.

There are two honest routes, and SiteSpot should offer both:

- **Route A — white-label / MVP** (brand an existing ordering+loyalty platform): **from ~120,000 Kč one-time + ~7,900 Kč/mo.** Live in ~6–10 weeks. Lowest risk, fastest ROI, some platform lock-in.
- **Route B — custom app** (own cross-platform build): **from ~390,000 Kč one-time + ~12,000 Kč/mo.** ~4–6 months. Fully owned, no per-order SaaS fees, no lock-in.

**Recommendation: to Ippa, quote the app "by agreement, once we know what they actually want it to do" — the number swings hugely with scope, so don't pin a figure up front.** The tiers below are SiteSpot's **internal guide** (roughly ~120–180k Kč for a lean MVP, ~390k+ for a custom build) so you know the ballpark and can steer the conversation — but the deck and the first call stay scope-first, not price-first.

White-label collapses the one-time cost to a **config + branding** fee instead of a full build.

At a blended **~1,000–1,300 Kč/h** studio rate → **≈ 460k–1.05M Kč**. A café-friendly, competitively-priced small-studio quote lands at **~390–650k Kč** for a well-scoped v1.

## 4. The three tiers to quote Ippa

### ⬦ Tier 1 — MVP / white-label · "Get it live"
**From 120,000 Kč one-time · from 7,900 Kč / mo · ~6–10 weeks**
Branded app (PWA + App Store + Google Play), order-ahead + pickup at all 4 branches, loyalty/stamps, gift cards, push, 3 languages, payments. Built on a proven white-label platform, configured and branded by SiteSpot.
*Best first step. Lowest risk, fastest to revenue.*

### ⬦ Tier 2 — Custom app · "Own it"  ★ upgrade path
**From 390,000 Kč one-time · from 12,000 Kč / mo · ~4–6 months**
Everything in Tier 1, built as a fully-owned cross-platform app: custom design, own backend/CMS, course & cake booking, wallet passes, deeper multi-branch logic, no per-order platform fees, no lock-in.

### ⬦ Tier 3 — Premium · "The full machine"
**From 690,000 Kč one-time · from 18,000 Kč / mo · ~6–9 months**
Adds the 24/7 AI concierge, monthly pastry-box subscriptions, deep POS + inventory + analytics, and advanced personalised push/segmentation.

*Plus pass-through: Apple $99/yr, Google $25 once. Monthly covers hosting, updates, store upkeep and support (≈ the 15–20%/yr maintenance benchmark).*

## 5. Payback sanity-check (illustrative, not a promise)

Order-ahead lifts spend ~25% and loyalty members spend ~20–32% more/yr (see `RESEARCH.md §7`). With 62k Instagram followers and 4 branches as the launch audience, even a **modest** shift of repeat business covers the monthly and amortises Tier 1 within a season. Tier 2/3 need real adoption to justify — which is exactly why we **prove demand on Tier 1 first**.

---

## 6. Risks & caveats

- **From-prices, not fixed quotes.** Final numbers depend on payment provider, POS reality, and how much content Ippa supplies. Re-scope before signing.
- **White-label = platform dependency** (monthly SaaS, possible per-order fees, limited deep customisation). Custom = higher upfront, full ownership.
- **An app is a big spend for a café.** It only pays back if Ippa actively drives installs (QR on boxes, in-store prompts, the 62k IG audience). If they won't push adoption, stay on web + Google/ordering first.
- Benchmarks are global aggregates; Czech-rate build costs are the relevant anchor and are labelled as estimates.

---

## 7. Recommendation

1. **Quote the app "by agreement," after a short scoping call — never a fixed number up front.** It depends entirely on features (payments? POS integration? wallet passes? the AI concierge?), so pin the *scope* first; the price follows. This is now how the pitch page phrases it too ("priced by agreement: it depends entirely on what you want it to do").
2. **Use the tiers as your own internal anchors** so you know where the conversation should land — ~120–180k Kč for a lean white-label MVP, ~390–650k for a custom build, more for premium — without quoting them verbatim.
3. **Keep the app off the front page as a headline price** — the deck deliberately sequences English first; talk numbers only when Ippa asks "and the app?", once the first win is on the board.
