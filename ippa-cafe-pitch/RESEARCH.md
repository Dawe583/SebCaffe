# Ippa Café — Market Research & Opportunity Brief

*Prepared for the SiteSpot pitch. Research pass run 2026-07-18 using the `market-research` skill.*

---

## 1. Executive summary

Ippa runs a genuinely international pastry business — the **International Prague Pastry Academy (IPPA)**, a café network, an e-shop and a corporate/teambuilding arm — on a **Czech-only website with no language switch**. Meanwhile the audience standing in front of it is overwhelmingly foreign: **Prague Castle drew a record ~2.7 million paying visitors in 2025, ~9 million through the grounds, and an autumn-2025 survey found 77 % of them were from abroad.**

The English website is therefore not a "nice to have" — it is the single biggest conversion leak, and it is table stakes: **direct Prague competitors (nasladko, Frutiko, Bakeshop, Sugar MaMa, Fairy Tale, Dortini) already ship English sites, online ordering and gift vouchers.** Ippa is behind peers, not ahead.

Beyond English, there is a credible, **sequenced** menu of higher-margin digital work — online course booking, a corporate/gifting portal, QR ordering, gift-card/loyalty in the phone wallet, reservation + voice-AI, and lifecycle email — each tied to a real Ippa asset and each with published impact benchmarks. **Recommendation: lead with English (the starting deal), then sell the Academy booking + corporate/gifting portal next, because those attach to Ippa's highest-margin, already-selling-out products.**

## 2. Key findings

### 2.1 The client (what Ippa actually is)
- **IPPA – International Prague Pastry Academy**, ~**550 m²** in the **Rosetta Palace, Jungmannova 30/21a, Prague 1**; presentation hall for **45**, hands-on workshop for **8**, dedicated **kids' hall (ages 7–15)**. [ippa-academy.cz, firmy.cz]
- **70+ courses**, incl. professional confectioner courses, **corporate culinary teambuilding**, kids' courses, and **guest masterclasses from world names (e.g. Amaury Guichon)**. [ippa-academy.cz]
- Operates as **café(s) + e-shop** (cakes, desserts, vouchers) alongside the academy; the pitch's own audit shows **4 branches incl. a Prague Castle location, a Czech-only site, no EN/DE, no language switch**, and an active Instagram presence.
- **Read-through:** this is not a corner cukrárna. It has high-margin SKUs (courses, bespoke cakes, corporate) that reward booking/e-commerce tooling far more than a plain café would.

### 2.2 The demand is foreign and already at the door (market sizing)
- **Prague Castle 2025: record ~2.7 M paying visitors; ~9 M through the grounds/gardens.** [Prague Daily News, expats.cz]
- **Autumn-2025 survey: 77 % of Castle visitors were foreign, 23 % Czech.** [expats.cz]
- Prague hotel/tourism volume overall is at record highs post-pandemic (millions of overnight guests/yr). [Statista, CzechTourism]
- **Implication:** a café/e-shop physically at the Castle sits in front of a majority-English-speaking flow and captures ~0 of it online today. Even a tiny conversion on a fraction of that traffic dwarfs the ~45k Kč starting fee.

### 2.3 Competitive landscape — English + e-commerce is the norm, not the edge
- **nasladko.cz** — English site, made-to-order cakes, online ordering, 15 pickup points + delivery.
- **Frutiko.cz** — English, full online patisserie, nationwide delivery, gift baskets.
- **Bakeshop.cz** — English, custom cakes, home/office/event delivery, free delivery >2,000 Kč.
- **Sugar MaMa / Fairy Tale Bakery / Dortini** — English sites, online cake ordering, **gift vouchers**.
- **Read-through:** Ippa's Czech-only site is a competitive *disadvantage* against smaller shops. This reframes the pitch from "upgrade" to "catch up, then leap."

### 2.4 Digital-commerce impact benchmarks (justify each menu item)
- **QR / digital ordering:** average **digital order value ~23 % higher than in-person**; visual QR menus reported to lift average check **~10–30 %**; QR payment ~15 % faster table turnover. *(Vendor/aggregator figures — directional.)* [Lightspeed, doXmenu, Supercode]
- **Missed calls / voice-AI:** restaurants miss **~43 % of calls**; **69 %** of customers give up if no one answers and **60 %** won't call back; voice-AI cuts missed calls **~87 %** at ~95 % accuracy (2025). *(Source is a voice-AI vendor — self-interested; treat as directional.)* [hostie.ai]
- **Lifecycle email:** email ROI commonly cited at **~$36–40 per $1**; abandoned-cart emails convert **~10–15 %**; cart abandonment averages **~70 %**. [Klaviyo, Rejoiner, Baymard-derived]
- **Gift cards:** global gift-card market **~$1.24 T (2025) → projected ~$4.23 T by 2035 (~13 % CAGR)**; digital gift cards growing **~17 % CAGR**, online channel ~61 % of issuance. [Precedence Research, The Business Research Company]

## 3. Implications — the expanded, sequenced offer

Each item is tied to a real Ippa asset. **★ = the starting deal. ⬦ NEW = added by this research pass.**

**Wave 1 — Speak every visitor's language**
- ★ Full **English** version of the whole site + e-shop (language switch on every page).
- German next (Prague's German/Austrian tourist base).
- Multilingual **QR menu** at the table (digital order value +~23 %).
- ⬦ NEW **Allergen & menu info in every language** (EU trust + tourist confidence at the counter).
- 24/7 multilingual **concierge chat**.

**Wave 2 — Get found before they arrive**
- **Google Business Profile** that sells (Maps, reserve/order, English reviews on top).
- Shoppable **Instagram**.
- **QR concierge cards** in nearby Castle-district hotels.
- Tourist-ready **branch finder** for the 4 locations.
- ⬦ NEW **TripAdvisor / review-engine** setup (tourists search reviews before they walk in).

**Wave 3 — Turn one visit into many**
- **QR on every cake box** → reorder / book a course / leave a review.
- **Reviews on the site** (five stars everywhere but their own page today).
- **Lifecycle email/SMS** — post-purchase, abandoned cart, birthday (email ~$36–40 / $1).
- **Gift cards + loyalty in Apple/Google Wallet** (gift-card market $1.24T→$4.23T).
- **Monthly pastry-box subscription** (recurring revenue; expats + offices).

**Wave 4 — Sell the high-margin things**
- **Academy: online booking + waitlists instead of discounts** (courses sell out, then get discounted 20–33 % to fill — stop giving margin away).
- **Design-your-own cake** with an instant quote (bespoke/wedding).
- **Corporate & gifting portal** — bulk vouchers, team courses, recurring office deliveries (teambuilding is already a live IPPA product).
- **Gift-an-experience** course vouchers (e-gift delivered instantly).
- ⬦ NEW **Masterclass & guest-chef ticketing + paid replays** (monetise names like Amaury Guichon beyond the room).

**Wave 5 — Run it like a machine**
- **Online table reservations** (63–70 % still prefer to call — give them both).
- **AI phone agent**, 24/7, any language (43 % of calls missed today).
- ⬦ NEW **WhatsApp order & booking line** (default tourist messaging channel).
- **Seasonal campaigns on autopilot** (Christmas / Easter / Valentine's).
- **One dashboard** — what sells, which courses fill, which branch.

## 4. Risks & caveats

- **Foot traffic ≠ customers.** The 9 M "grounds" figure is not café buyers; use the 2.7 M *paying* / 77 % foreign framing, and treat any revenue math as illustrative, not promised.
- **Several impact stats are vendor-sourced** (hostie.ai for voice-AI, QR-menu vendors for check uplift). They are directional, not guarantees — labelled as such above.
- **Ippa's real site may already have partial i18n or an agency contract.** The pitch must stay "we add, we don't replace," and the audit ("no language switch") should be re-confirmed on the live site before send.
- **Don't over-scope the first sell.** The strength of the pitch is sequencing — one win (English), proven, then the next. Presenting all 20+ items as one project reads as an agency land-grab.
- **Gift-card / market-size figures are global**, included for direction of travel, not a Prague-specific TAM.

---

## 5. Recommendation

1. **Lead with English** as the wedge — it is table stakes, competitors already have it, and the Castle audience is 77 % foreign. Keep the ~45k Kč starting price.
2. **Name the next two explicitly** (Academy booking + waitlists, and the Corporate/gifting portal) because they attach to Ippa's highest-margin, already-selling-out products — that is where the follow-on revenue and the retainer live.
3. **Keep the full menu visible but sequenced** ("you pick the next one"), so Ippa sees the roadmap without feeling sold a mega-project.
4. Re-confirm the live-site audit before sending, and keep every number on the page sourced.
